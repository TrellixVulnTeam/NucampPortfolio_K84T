Many years ago, the creator of this web site was a graduate student in a geology program in the southwestern United States. She had to come up with a research topic for her dissertation. Being a new mother, she wanted to choose a field study area close to home. When hiking in the beautiful mountains and desert, she would often wonder, what research has been done in this area? What research topics are there? What are the open research questions related to this area. Having a background in GIS, she thought “Wouldn’t it be great if I could just choose an area on a map and get a list of all the geology papers done on this area? Wouldn’t it be great if I could narrow the search by keyword, year, author, etc”

Fast forward to the present day where she is a student in the Nucamp and has to choose her first portfolio project….An Open-Source GIS Map server containing GIS (spatial) data that represents an area covered in geology papers. The user can query on an internet map server and get a list of geology papers related to the area. Enter in keywords (e.g., volcano, structure, quaternary, paleontology), authors, year, etc., to narrow the search. Download a list of references, and in some cases, the document itself.

First, I have to get some data. I will ask some of my colleagues, friends, and acquaintances to upload their references and associated spatial data to a database so it can be queried. Hopefully, the idea will catch on, and publishers will provide their data as well. Wish me luck!
